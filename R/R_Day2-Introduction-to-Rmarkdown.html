Normal distribution

A variable \(X\) is normally distributed, or \(X\sim N(\mu, \sigma^2)\), if:

\[ f_X(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma^2}}\exp\left\{-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right\}, \quad x\in\mathbb{R}. \] Note that \(\mu\) and \(\sigma\) are respectively population mean and standard deviation.
